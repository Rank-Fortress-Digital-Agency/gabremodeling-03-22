The Different Types of Home Remodeling

Read Post

There are many different types of home remodeling, and it can be hard to know where to start. Whether you’re looking to update your kitchen or bathroom, add a new room, or completely renovate your home, there’s a lot to consider. To help you get started, we’ve put together a list of the different types of home remodeling.


  • Kitchen Remodeling: This is one of the most popular types of home remodeling. If you’re looking to update your kitchen, there are a few things to consider, such as new cabinets, countertops, appliances, and flooring.

  • Bathroom Remodeling: Another popular type of home remodeling is bathroom remodeling. If you’re looking to update your bathroom, there are a few things to consider, such as new fixtures, vanities, toilets, and showers.

  • Room Additions: If you’re looking to add an extra room to your home, there are a few things to consider, such as the size of the room and what’ll use it for.

  • Home Renovations: If you’re looking to completely renovate your home, there are a few things to consider, such as the style of your home, the layout of your rooms, and what materials you want to use.

How Wide Should My Hallways Be?

This is a common question that people have when they are remodeling their homes. The answer to this question depends on how you plan to use your hallways. If you plan to use your hallway as the main thoroughfare, then it should be at least three feet wide. However, if you only plan to use your hallway for passing through, then two feet wide will suffice. Remember to take into account the width of doorways when planning the width of your hallways.

Possible costs for Home Remodeling

The cost of home remodeling can vary depending on the project. However, there are some general costs that you can expect:

  • Labor: This will typically be your biggest expense when remodeling your home. Depending on the contractor you choose and the type of work that needs to be done, labor can range from $25 to $50 per hour.

  • Materials: The cost of materials will depend on the specific project you are undertaking. For example, a new kitchen countertop may cost around $100, while a new roof could cost thousands of dollars.

  • Permits and inspections: In most cases, you will need to obtain permits and have inspections performed during your home remodeling project. These typically cost between $50 and $200, depending on your location.


If you are planning a home remodeling project, it is important to factor in all of the potential costs before getting started. By doing so, you can ensure that you stay within your budget and avoid any unexpected expenses.

Additional costs that are often forgotten about:

  • Contingency fund: This is money set aside for unforeseen circumstances that may arise during the project. It is typically recommended to have at least 20% of the total project cost set aside in a contingency fund.

  • Change orders: These are costs that come up when changes are made to the scope of work during the project. Change orders can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand, depending on the size and nature of the change.

  • Fees: There may be fees associated with your home remodelings project, such as permits or inspections. These should be factored into your budget as well.

  • When budgeting for your home remodeling project, be sure to include all potential costs so you can avoid any surprises down the road. By doing so, you can ensure that your project stays on track and within budget.
